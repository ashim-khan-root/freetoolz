Unit Conversion Guide: How to Convert Length, Mass & Temperature

June 22, 2026 · 6 min read

Whether you’re cooking a foreign recipe, planning a road trip, shipping a package, or working on a DIY project, converting between measurement units is something everyone faces. Metric and imperial systems coexist in daily life — and switching between them shouldn’t require a math degree.

This guide covers the most common conversions for length, mass, and temperature, with quick reference tables you can bookmark and come back to.

Length Conversion

Length is the most frequently converted measurement. From measuring furniture to calculating travel distances, here’s everything you need.

Metric to Imperial

Metric Imperial How to Convert
1 mm 0.039 inches mm × 0.039 = inches
1 cm 0.394 inches cm × 0.394 = inches
1 m 3.281 feet m × 3.281 = feet
1 m 1.094 yards m × 1.094 = yards
1 km 0.621 miles km × 0.621 = miles

Imperial to Metric

Imperial Metric How to Convert
1 inch 2.54 cm inches × 2.54 = cm
1 foot 0.305 m feet × 0.305 = m
1 yard 0.914 m yards × 0.914 = m
1 mile 1.609 km miles × 1.609 = km

Quick Mental Shortcuts

  • Inches to cm: Multiply by 2.5 (close enough for most purposes). 8 inches ≈ 20 cm (exact: 20.32 cm)
  • Feet to meters: Divide by 3. 12 feet ≈ 4 m (exact: 3.66 m)
  • Miles to km: Multiply by 1.6. 60 mph ≈ 96 km/h (exact: 96.56 km/h)
  • Km to miles: Multiply by 0.6. 100 km ≈ 60 miles (exact: 62.1 miles)

Mass Conversion

Mass (commonly called weight) conversions are essential for cooking, shipping, fitness, and international trade.

Metric to Imperial

Metric Imperial How to Convert
1 g 0.035 ounces g × 0.035 = oz
1 kg 2.205 pounds kg × 2.205 = lbs
1 tonne 1.102 US tons tonnes × 1.102 = US tons

Imperial to Metric

Imperial Metric How to Convert
1 ounce 28.35 g oz × 28.35 = g
1 pound 0.454 kg lbs × 0.454 = kg
1 US ton 0.907 tonnes US tons × 0.907 = tonnes

Quick Mental Shortcuts

  • Kg to pounds: Double it and add 10%. 70 kg → 140 + 14 = 154 lbs (exact: 154.3 lbs)
  • Pounds to kg: Halve it and subtract 10%. 154 lbs → 77 - 7.7 = 69.3 kg (exact: 69.9 kg)
  • Grams to ounces: Divide by 28 (rough). 250 g ≈ 8.9 oz (exact: 8.82 oz)

Shipping container max 30 tonnes 33 US tons

Temperature Conversion

Temperature conversions trip people up because the formulas are less intuitive than length or mass.

The Formulas

°C to °F: (°C × 9/5) + 32 = °F
°F to °C: (°F - 32) × 5/9 = °C

°C to K: °C + 273.15 = K
K to °C: K - 273.15 = °C

Quick Mental Shortcuts

  • °C to °F: Double it and add 30. 30°C → 60 + 30 = 90°F (exact: 86°F)
  • °F to °C: Subtract 30 and halve it. 90°F → 60 ÷ 2 = 30°C (exact: 32.2°C)
  • These shortcuts are accurate enough for weather, not for cooking or science.

Why Accurate Conversion Matters

Small conversion errors can compound into big problems:

  • Baking: A 10% error in flour (using 110g instead of 120g) can ruin a cake’s texture
  • Shipping: Miscalculating package dimensions by 2 inches can double shipping costs
  • Travel: Confusing miles and kilometers can lead to running out of fuel
  • Medicine: A temperature conversion error can mean the difference between a normal reading and a fever
  • Construction: A 0.5 cm error over 10 meters becomes a 5 cm gap at the end

When precision matters, use a conversion tool rather than mental math.

FAQ

How do I convert inches to cm? Multiply inches by 2.54. For example, 6 inches × 2.54 = 15.24 cm.

What’s the easiest way to convert Fahrenheit to Celsius? Subtract 30, then divide by 2. For weather temperatures, this shortcut is accurate enough. For exact conversion, use (°F - 32) × 5/9.

How many meters in a foot? One foot equals 0.3048 meters. For quick estimates, divide feet by 3.

What’s the difference between metric tonnes and US tons? A metric tonne = 1,000 kg. A US (short) ton = 907 kg. A UK (long) ton = 1,016 kg. The difference matters in shipping and trade.

How do I convert kg to pounds without a calculator? Double the kg value and add 10%. For 50 kg: 100 + 5 = 110 lbs (exact: 110.2 lbs).

Is 1 inch exactly 2.54 cm? Yes. Since 1959, the inch has been internationally defined as exactly 2.54 cm.
