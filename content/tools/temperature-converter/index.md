## FAQ

### How do I convert Celsius to Fahrenheit?
Multiply the Celsius temperature by 9/5 and add 32. For example: 30°C × 9/5 + 32 = 86°F. The converter does this math instantly.

### What is absolute zero in Celsius and Fahrenheit?
Absolute zero, the theoretical lowest possible temperature, is -273.15°C, -459.67°F, and 0 K. At this point, molecular motion would theoretically stop completely.

### Why does the US use Fahrenheit?
The Fahrenheit scale was developed in the early 18th century and became standard in English-speaking countries. Most of the world adopted Celsius (centigrade) as part of the metric system, but the US maintained Fahrenheit for everyday use.

### At what temperature are Celsius and Fahrenheit equal?
-40 degrees. At -40°C, the equivalent temperature is exactly -40°F. This is the only point where the two scales read the same value.

### Can I convert negative temperatures correctly?
Yes. The converter handles negative temperatures on all three scales correctly. For example, -10°C converts to 14°F and 263.15 K.

## Tips

- For approximate conversions, remember: Celsius to Fahrenheit ≈ double it and add 30 (rough, for weather temps)
- Water freezes at 0°C / 32°F / 273.15 K and boils at 100°C / 212°F / 373.15 K at sea level — useful mental reference points
- Room temperature is approximately 20-22°C (68-72°F)
- When cooking, a 350°F oven equals about 175°C — the most common baking temperature
- Kelvin has no negative values and is used primarily in scientific contexts — 0 K is absolute zero
