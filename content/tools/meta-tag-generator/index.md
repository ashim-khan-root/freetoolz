## FAQ

### What is the ideal meta title length?
Google typically displays the first 50-60 characters of a title tag. Keep your title under 60 characters to avoid truncation in search results.

### How long should a meta description be?
The ideal meta description length is 150-160 characters. Descriptions longer than this may be truncated in search results, though Google sometimes rewrites them entirely.

### Do I need both OG tags and Twitter Cards?
Yes. Facebook, LinkedIn, and Slack use Open Graph tags. Twitter uses its own Twitter Card tags. Having both ensures your content looks great everywhere it's shared.

### What is the og:image recommended size?
For best results, use an image that is at least 1200 x 630 pixels for Open Graph and 1200 x 600 pixels for Twitter Cards. Use a 1.91:1 aspect ratio.

### Can I use this for an existing page?
Yes. If your page already has meta tags, paste them in to see a preview. If not, generate new ones and replace the existing tags in your HTML head.

## Tips

- Place your primary keyword near the beginning of the title tag for better SEO impact
- Each page should have a unique meta description — avoid duplicating across your site
- Use action-oriented language in descriptions to improve click-through rates (CTR)
