## FAQ

### What is the ideal title tag length?
Google typically displays the first 50-70 characters of a title tag, or approximately 600 pixels on desktop. Keep your primary keywords within the first 50 characters and your full title under 70 characters to avoid truncation.

### Why does my meta description show fewer characters than expected?
Google calculates pixel width, not character count. Wide characters like "W" and uppercase letters take more space, while narrow characters like "i" and "l" take less. The preview shows you the exact pixel width rendering.

### Does the URL in search results affect SEO?
The breadcrumb URL path shown in Google results is based on your page URL structure. Clean, descriptive URLs with relevant keywords can improve click-through rates but are a minor ranking factor.

### Should I include my brand name in the title?
Including your brand name at the end of the title (e.g., "Keyword-Rich Title | Brand Name") is a common practice that helps with brand recognition and can prevent title rewriting by Google.

## Tips

- Front-load your most important keywords in the title tag — the first words carry the most weight
- Write meta descriptions that include a call to action or value proposition to boost click-through rate
- Use the mobile preview to check that your title doesn't get cut off on smaller screens
- Keep your primary keyword phrase within the first 120 characters of your meta description
