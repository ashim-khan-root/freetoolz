## FAQ

### What is BMR?
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at complete rest to maintain basic functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production. It accounts for about 60-75% of your daily calorie expenditure.

### What is TDEE?
TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is your total calorie burn including BMR, physical activity, digestion, and daily movement. Your macro targets are based on TDEE, not BMR, so selecting the right activity level is important.

### Is the macro split customizable?
The default split of 30/40/30 (protein/carbs/fat) is a balanced starting point for most people. Depending on your specific goals or dietary preferences, you may want to adjust these ratios — consult with a nutritionist for a personalized plan.

### Why is the weight loss deficit 500 calories?
A 500-calorie daily deficit typically results in about 1 pound (0.45 kg) of weight loss per week, which is considered a safe, sustainable rate. A larger deficit can cause muscle loss and metabolic slowdown.

### How accurate is this calculator?
The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is accurate within about 10% of measured BMR for most people. Individual variations in metabolism, muscle mass, and hormones mean your actual needs may differ. Use the results as a starting point and adjust based on real-world results.

## Tips

- Recalculate your macros every 10-15 pounds of weight change — your BMR changes as your body weight changes
- Protein intake of 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight is recommended for those trying to build muscle
- Don't drop below 1,200 calories (women) or 1,500 calories (men) without medical supervision
- Track your actual intake for 1-2 weeks and adjust based on whether you're losing, maintaining, or gaining as expected
