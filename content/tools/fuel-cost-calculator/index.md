## How to Use the Fuel Cost Calculator

1. **Enter trip distance** — type the total distance for your trip in kilometers or miles
2. **Set fuel efficiency** — enter your vehicle's fuel economy in km/L or MPG
3. **Enter fuel price** — the current price per liter or per gallon in your local currency
4. **Select fuel type** — choose petrol, diesel, CNG, or electric to see efficiency comparisons against averages for that fuel type
5. **Add commute details (optional)** — enter the number of trips per month and one-way distance to see your monthly commuting cost
6. **Review results** — see total fuel cost, fuel required in liters, cost per kilometer, and monthly commute expense

## Features

- **Total fuel cost** — the estimated cost of fuel for the trip you entered
- **Fuel required** — liters or gallons needed for the given distance
- **Cost per km** — per-kilometer (or per-mile) running cost of your vehicle
- **Monthly commute cost** — calculated based on trips per month and one-way distance
- **Efficiency comparison** — see how your vehicle's fuel efficiency compares to the average for its fuel type
- **Multi fuel type** — supports petrol (gasoline), diesel, CNG, and electric
- **Unit flexibility** — switch between km/liter and MPG based on your region

## FAQ

### How do I know my vehicle's fuel efficiency?
Check your car's owner's manual, the manufacturer specifications, or calculate it manually: fill the tank fully, note the odometer reading, drive normally, fill up again, and divide the distance driven by the liters filled. Most modern cars also show average consumption on the dashboard.

### Should I use km/L or MPG?
Use whichever unit you're familiar with. km/L is common in Asia, Europe, Australia, and many other regions. MPG (miles per gallon) is used in the US and UK. The calculator supports both — just toggle between them.

### What about electric vehicles (EVs)?
For EVs, enter the electricity cost per kWh as the "fuel price" and use the efficiency rating from your EV (e.g., 6 km/kWh or 3.5 mi/kWh). The calculator treats it the same way — you'll see the cost per km and total trip cost.

### Does this account for traffic, AC usage, or driving style?
No — the calculator uses the efficiency number you enter, which represents average conditions. Real-world fuel consumption is higher in city traffic, when using AC, or with aggressive driving. For a more accurate estimate, use a slightly lower efficiency value than the manufacturer's claim.

### How can I reduce my fuel costs?
Drive smoothly (avoid rapid acceleration and hard braking), maintain proper tire pressure, remove roof racks when not in use, use AC wisely (it increases consumption by 5-20%), and combine short trips into one longer trip to avoid repeated cold-engine starts.
