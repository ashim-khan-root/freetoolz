## FAQ

### What is a healthy BMI?
A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy (normal weight range). Below 18.5 is underweight, 25-29.9 is overweight, and 30+ is obese.

### Can BMI be inaccurate for athletes?
Yes. BMI doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes and bodybuilders with high muscle mass may have a high BMI despite being very healthy. Use the body fat calculator for a more accurate picture.

### Is BMI different for men and women?
The same BMI ranges apply to both men and women. However, women typically have higher body fat percentages than men at the same BMI.

### How often should I check my BMI?
Once a month is sufficient for tracking general trends. BMI changes slowly, so daily checks won't show meaningful differences.

## Tips

- Take BMI measurements at the same time of day (morning is best) for consistent tracking
- BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic — use it alongside other health metrics
- For children and teens, use age-and-sex-specific BMI percentile charts rather than adult categories
