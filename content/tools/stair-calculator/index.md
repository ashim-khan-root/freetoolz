## How to Use the Stair Calculator

1. **Enter total rise** — measure the total vertical height from the finished floor below to the finished floor above
2. **Adjust target riser height** — the optimal riser height is around 7 inches (most comfortable for walking)
3. **Set tread depth** — minimum 10 inches for residential stairs (11 inches is more comfortable)
4. **Enter nosing projection** — if your treads have an overhang, enter it (typically 0.75 to 1.25 inches)
5. **Get results** — see the number of steps, actual riser height, total run, and exact stringer length for cutting

Perfect for builders, contractors, and DIY staircase construction. Follows standard 7-11 stair guidelines. All calculations in your browser.

## FAQ

### What's the ideal riser height for comfortable stairs?
A 7-inch riser height is considered the most comfortable for most people. The IRC code allows up to 7.75 inches. The calculator helps you find the best riser height that divides evenly into your total rise.

### What is the minimum tread depth?
The IRC requires a minimum 10-inch tread depth for residential stairs. For commercial stairs, the minimum is 11 inches. Deeper treads (11-12 inches) are more comfortable, especially for people with larger feet.

### How much headroom do I need?
Building codes typically require at least 6 feet 8 inches (80 inches) of headroom measured vertically from the nosing of each tread. The calculator alerts you if your design doesn't provide enough clearance.

### Can this calculator help with spiral stairs?
This calculator is designed for straight-run stairs. Spiral stairs have different code requirements and geometry. For spiral stairs, consult a specialist or use a dedicated spiral stair calculator.

### What is a stringer and how many do I need?
A stringer is the angled board that supports the treads and risers. For most residential stairs up to 36 inches wide, you need three stringers (one on each side and one in the center). For wider stairs, add more stringers.

## Tips

- Always verify field measurements before cutting — total rise can vary by an inch or more from architectural plans
- Build a temporary stringer from scrap plywood first to test the fit before cutting your final stringer boards
- Keep all riser heights within 3/8 inch of each other — variations larger than this are a tripping hazard
- Add handrails on both sides for stairs wider than 44 inches as required by most building codes
