## How to Use the List Sorter

1. **Paste your list** — enter one item per line in the text area
2. **Choose sort type** — alphabetical (A-Z), reverse alphabetical (Z-A), or numeric sorting
3. **Toggle options** — enable "Remove duplicates" and "Trim whitespace" for cleaner results
4. **Click Sort** — your sorted list appears instantly in the output area
5. **Copy the result** — one click copies the sorted list to your clipboard

Perfect for organizing shopping lists, sorting names, ordering data, deduplicating entries, and preparing data for analysis.

## FAQ

### Can I sort numbers mixed with text?
Yes. Choose "Alphabetical" for mixed content — numbers will be sorted as strings. For pure number lists where you want 2 before 10, choose "Numeric" sort.

### Will it remove blank lines?
Yes, blank lines are automatically removed during sorting regardless of which options you select.

### Is my data stored anywhere?
No. Everything runs in your browser locally. Your data never reaches our server and is cleared when you refresh the page.

### What's the difference between alphabetical and numeric sorting?
Alphabetical sort treats items as text — "10" comes before "2" because "1" < "2". Numeric sort treats items as numbers — 2 comes before 10. Always use numeric sort for number-only lists.

### Can I sort by the second word in each line?
Currently the tool sorts by the entire line content. For advanced sorting needs, consider using a spreadsheet application.

## Tips

- Enable "Trim whitespace" before deduplicating to catch duplicates with inconsistent spacing
- Sort alphabetically first, then remove duplicates, for the cleanest results
- Use numeric sort for price lists, ages, scores, or any purely numerical data
