## FAQ

### What is BMI Prime?
BMI Prime is the ratio of your actual BMI to the upper limit of the normal range (25). A BMI Prime of 1.0 means you're exactly at the boundary between normal and overweight. Below 1.0 is healthy, above 1.0 indicates excess weight.

### Why is BMI Prime better than regular BMI?
BMI Prime is more intuitive. Instead of remembering "normal is 18.5-24.9," you just need to know that 1.0 or less is healthy. A BMI Prime of 1.2 means you're 20% above the normal limit.

### How is BMI Prime calculated?
BMI Prime = your BMI / 25. So if your BMI is 30, your BMI Prime is 30/25 = 1.2. This means your BMI is 20% above the upper healthy limit.

### Does BMI Prime have the same limitations as regular BMI?
Yes. Like BMI, BMI Prime doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition. It's best used as a screening tool alongside other health metrics.
