## FAQ

### How accurate is the due date estimate?
Only about 4% of babies are born on their exact due date. The due date is an estimate — full-term pregnancy ranges from 37 to 42 weeks. The calculator gives a statistically reasonable estimate based on standard medical formulas.

### What's the difference between LMP and conception date methods?
The LMP method adds 280 days (40 weeks) from the first day of your last period. The conception date method adds 266 days (38 weeks) from the date of conception. For most women with regular 28-day cycles, both methods give similar results.

### Can I calculate based on IVF transfer date?
This calculator uses LMP and conception methods. If you had an IVF transfer, your fertility clinic will provide a due date based on the transfer date and embryo age (day 3 or day 5). Consult your clinic for the most accurate calculation.

### What if my cycle is not 28 days?
The standard 280-day calculation assumes a 28-day cycle. For longer or shorter cycles, the due date may shift. Talk to your healthcare provider about adjusting your due date based on your cycle length or early ultrasound measurements.

### Does this replace a doctor's confirmation?
No — this is an informational tool only. Always confirm your due date with a healthcare provider through ultrasound and clinical evaluation. Early ultrasound dating in the first trimester is the most accurate method.
