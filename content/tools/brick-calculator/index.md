## FAQ

### How many bricks do I need per square foot?
For a standard modular brick (3-5/8" × 2-1/4") with a 3/8" mortar joint, you need approximately 7 bricks per square foot of wall surface. This varies by brick size and joint thickness.

### Should I order extra bricks?
Yes. Always add at least 10% for waste, breakage, and cuts. It's better to have leftover bricks than to run out mid-project and struggle with color matching from a different batch.

### What's the difference between a brick and a block?
Bricks are smaller, typically 2-1/4" thick, and designed for walls, façades, and pavements. Blocks (CMU — Concrete Masonry Units) are much larger, typically 8×8×16 inches, used for structural walls and foundations.

### Do I need mortar for every type of project?
Most permanent brick structures use mortar. For patios and walkways, you can use sand-set installation (mortarless) or mortar-set for greater stability. The calculator includes mortar estimates for mortared projects.

### How do I measure for a non-rectangular wall?
Break the wall into rectangular sections, calculate each separately, and add the totals. For arches or triangles, estimate generously and add extra waste allowance.

## Tips

- Order all bricks from the same batch to ensure consistent color — different batches vary noticeably
- Store bricks off the ground and covered with a tarp to protect them from weather before installation
- For large projects, have a few extra bricks on hand for future repairs — matching existing brick is very difficult
