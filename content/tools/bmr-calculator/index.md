## FAQ

### What is BMR?
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at complete rest to maintain vital functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production. It accounts for roughly 60-75% of your total daily energy expenditure.

### What is the Mifflin-St Jeor equation?
It's the most accurate formula for estimating BMR. For men: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) + 5. For women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) - 161.

### How accurate is this BMR estimate?
The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is accurate to within about 100-200 calories per day for most people. Individual metabolic rates vary based on genetics, muscle mass, and hormones.

### What's the difference between BMR and TDEE?
BMR is your resting calorie burn. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is BMR plus all activity — walking, exercise, digestion. Your maintenance calories = TDEE.

### Does BMR change with age?
Yes. BMR decreases by roughly 1-2% per decade after age 20, primarily due to loss of muscle mass. Strength training helps maintain a higher BMR as you age.

## Tips

- For weight loss, subtract 300-500 calories from your maintenance level for sustainable fat loss
- For muscle gain, add 200-300 calories above maintenance — more will add excess fat
- Recalculate your BMR every 10-15 lbs of weight change, as your numbers will shift
