## FAQ

### How do you convert km/h to mph?
Multiply the speed in km/h by 0.621371. For example, 100 km/h × 0.621371 = 62.14 mph. This converter does the math for you instantly.

### What is a knot and when is it used?
A knot is one nautical mile per hour (1 knot = 1.852 km/h). It's used primarily in aviation, maritime navigation, and meteorology for wind speeds at sea.

### Is mach speed constant?
No. Mach 1 varies with temperature and altitude. This converter uses Mach 1 = 1,235 km/h at sea level and 20°C. At higher altitudes where it's colder, Mach 1 is slower.

### Why does the converter show 4 decimal places?
Four decimal places provides sufficient precision for most practical uses. For everyday conversions like driving speeds, the first decimal place is usually enough. Engineering applications benefit from the extra precision.

## Tips

- For driving, remember that 100 km/h ≈ 62 mph and 60 mph ≈ 97 km/h as quick reference points
- A nautical mile is about 15% longer than a statute mile, which is why knots are faster than mph despite sounding similar
- When measuring wind for sailing, always use knots — it's the universal standard in marine forecasts
- For quick mental conversions: km/h to mph ≈ divide by 1.6, mph to km/h ≈ multiply by 1.6
