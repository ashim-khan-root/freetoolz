## FAQ

### How many calories should I eat to lose weight?
A deficit of 300-500 calories below maintenance typically results in 0.5-1 lb of weight loss per week. The calculator adjusts your target based on your goal pace — choose a smaller deficit for sustainable loss or a larger one for faster results.

### What are macronutrients?
Macronutrients are protein, carbohydrates, and fat. Protein and carbs provide 4 calories per gram, fat provides 9 calories per gram. The calculator splits your daily calories into these three categories based on your goals.

### How do I track my macros?
Use a food tracking app or read nutrition labels. Weigh your food with a kitchen scale for accuracy. Focus on hitting your protein target first, then adjust carbs and fat to stay within your calorie goal.

### Should I eat back exercise calories?
Generally, no. Activity levels in the calculator already account for your typical movement. Eating back exercise calories often eliminates the deficit needed for weight loss.

### Is this calculator suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women?
The calculator uses general formulas. Pregnancy and breastfeeding increase calorie needs significantly. Consult your healthcare provider for personalized recommendations during these periods.

## Tips

- Protein is the most important macro to hit — aim for at least 0.8-1g per pound of body weight
- Weigh and log your food for at least 2 weeks to understand your actual eating patterns
- Recalculate every 10-15 lbs of weight change, as your calorie needs decrease as you lose weight
- Use the pie chart visualization to quickly check if your daily meal plan matches your target macro split
