## FAQ

### What happens if I wake up in the middle of a sleep cycle?
Waking up during deep sleep (mid-cycle) causes sleep inertia — that groggy, disoriented feeling that can last 30-60 minutes. This calculator helps you schedule sleep so you wake at the end of a cycle when you're in light sleep.

### Why 90 minutes per cycle?
Sleep cycles average 90 minutes for most adults. A full cycle includes light sleep (N1, N2), deep sleep (N3), and REM sleep. Waking at the end of REM or light sleep leaves you feeling most refreshed.

### Can I use this calculator for power naps?
Yes. For a 20-minute power nap, choose the 14-minute fall-asleep buffer with a target wake time 20 minutes later. For a full-cycle nap, aim for 90 minutes. Avoid waking mid-cycle during naps too.

### Is the 14-minute fall-asleep buffer accurate for everyone?
No — it's the average. Some people fall asleep in 5 minutes, others take 30. Adjust the buffer based on your personal experience for the most accurate recommendations.

## Tips

- Be consistent — going to bed and waking at the same time (even on weekends) strengthens your circadian rhythm
- Avoid screens for 30-60 minutes before your calculated bedtime — blue light suppresses melatonin
- Try both modes: if you're flexible on bedtime, use wake-up mode; if you have a fixed bedtime, use bedtime mode
- Combine this calculator with a sleep tracker app to see how close your actual cycles match the 90-minute average
