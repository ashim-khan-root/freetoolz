## FAQ

### What makes a password "Very Strong"?
A Very Strong password typically has at least 16 characters, includes all four character types (uppercase, lowercase, digits, symbols), contains no common words or patterns, and achieves high entropy (70+ bits).

### What is entropy in password security?
Entropy measures how unpredictable a password is, expressed in bits. Each bit doubles the number of possible combinations. A password with 80 bits of entropy would take billions of years to crack by brute force — it is effectively unbreakable through guessing alone.

### Does the checker store my password?
No. All analysis happens in your browser using JavaScript. The password you type never leaves your device. We do not log, store, or transmit any data from the checker.

### How does the checker detect common passwords?
The tool includes a built-in list of the most common and frequently breached passwords (like "123456," "password," "qwerty"). If your password matches an entry in this list, it is flagged immediately regardless of length or character variety.

### What should I do if my password is Weak?
Increase the length first — every additional character dramatically improves strength. Then add symbols and numbers. Avoid dictionary words, personal information, and sequential patterns. A password manager can help generate and store strong passwords.

## Tips

- Aim for at least 16 characters — length is the single most important factor in password strength
- Use a passphrase (4-5 random words strung together) — they are easier to remember and very strong
- Never reuse passwords across different sites — a breach on one site compromises all your accounts
