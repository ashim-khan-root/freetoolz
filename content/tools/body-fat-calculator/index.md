## FAQ

### How accurate is the US Navy method?
The US Navy body fat method has an accuracy of about 2-3% compared to DEXA scans or hydrostatic weighing. It's one of the most accurate measurement-based estimation methods available without specialized equipment.

### What measurements do I need?
For the US Navy method, you need: neck circumference, waist circumference (at navel level), and hip circumference (for women only). Measure at the widest point for consistent results.

### What are healthy body fat percentages?
For men: 6-24% is healthy (athletes 6-13%, fit 14-17%, average 18-24%). For women: 16-30% is healthy (athletes 16-23%, fit 24-27%, average 28-30%). Essential fat minimums are 2-5% for men and 10-13% for women.

### How often should I measure body fat?
Once every 2-4 weeks is sufficient. Body fat changes slowly, and daily measurements show too much fluctuation due to hydration, food intake, and measurement consistency.

## Tips

- Take measurements at the same time of day, ideally in the morning before eating
- Use a flexible measuring tape and keep it level — not too tight or too loose
- The US Navy method is most accurate for people with average body types; very lean or very muscular individuals may get less accurate results
