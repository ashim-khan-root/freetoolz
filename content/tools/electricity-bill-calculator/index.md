## FAQ

### How accurate is the calculator compared to my actual bill?
The calculator gives a reasonable estimate based on the appliances and usage hours you enter. Actual bills may vary due to seasonal usage changes, voltage fluctuations, utility rounding, and additional charges like taxes that are not included here.

### What are slab rates in electricity billing?
Slab rates mean the per-unit price changes based on how much electricity you consume. For example, the first 100 units might cost $0.05/unit, next 200 units cost $0.07/unit, and anything above costs $0.10/unit. This is common in residential billing worldwide.

### Why is my AC the biggest power consumer?
Air conditioners typically draw 1,000-2,500 watts while running, far more than most other household appliances. A 1.5-ton AC running 8 hours/day can easily account for 40-60% of your total electricity bill in hot climates.

### Does standby power consumption matter?
Yes — devices on standby (TVs, set-top boxes, chargers, game consoles) draw small amounts of power continuously. While each device is 1-10W, a household with 15 such devices wastes 100-150W around the clock — roughly 72-108 kWh per month.

### What's the difference between kW and kWh?
kW (kilowatt) is a measure of power — the rate at which an appliance uses electricity at a given moment. kWh (kilowatt-hour) is energy — the total amount used over time. A 1,000W AC running for 1 hour consumes 1 kWh. Your bill charges per kWh.
