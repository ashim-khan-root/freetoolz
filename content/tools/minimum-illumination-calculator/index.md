## FAQ

### What is lux?
Lux is the SI unit of illuminance, measuring how much light falls on a surface. One lux equals one lumen per square meter. For reference, a full moon on a clear night provides about 0.1 lux.

### What is a good minimum illumination rating?
For outdoor night surveillance, look for cameras rated at 0.01 lux or lower in color mode and 0.005 lux or lower in B&W mode. Higher numbers mean the camera needs more light to produce a usable image.

### Does a lower lux rating always mean better?
Generally yes, but also consider the sensor size and image processing. A camera rated at 0.001 lux with a large sensor will outperform one rated at 0.001 lux with a small sensor at the same light level.

### Should I use color or B&W mode at night?
B&W/IR mode is typically 3x more sensitive than color mode because the color filter array is removed from the imaging path. If you need nighttime performance, IR mode is almost always better.

### Can I measure light levels without a lux meter?
For rough estimates, use the scene presets in the calculator. For accurate measurements, use a smartphone app with a light meter feature or a dedicated lux meter.

## Tips

- Always test the actual installation site at night before finalizing camera placement — the calculator gives a theoretical rating, but real-world conditions vary
- Account for seasonal changes in ambient light — winter nights may be darker or longer
- Consider using IR illuminators to improve scene lighting if the calculator shows "marginal" or "insufficient"
