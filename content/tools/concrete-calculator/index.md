## FAQ

### How much does a yard of concrete weigh?
A cubic yard of concrete weighs approximately 4,000 pounds (1.8 metric tons). This varies slightly by mix design but is the standard estimate used in construction.

### Should I order extra concrete?
Yes — always order 5-10% more than calculated to account for spillage, uneven subgrades, and slight depth variations. The calculator doesn't add waste automatically, so factor this in.

### How many 80lb bags make a yard?
It takes approximately 45 bags of 80lb concrete mix to make one cubic yard. The calculator breaks this down automatically for each bag size.

### Can I pour concrete in cold weather?
Concrete can be poured in temperatures as low as 25°F (-4°C) using accelerators and insulating blankets, but the ideal range is 50-70°F (10-21°C). Below 25°F, fresh concrete can freeze and lose strength permanently.
