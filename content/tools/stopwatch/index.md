## How to Use the Stopwatch

1. **Click Start** to begin timing — the display starts counting up immediately
2. **Click Lap** to record a lap time while the stopwatch continues running in the background
3. **Click Stop** to pause the timer at the current elapsed time
4. **Click Reset** to clear all times and start fresh
5. **Copy laps** — export your recorded lap times to clipboard for pasting into a spreadsheet or document

Perfect for tracking workout times, measuring task durations, timing presentations, or any activity that needs precise timing.

## FAQ

### How accurate is the stopwatch?
The stopwatch uses JavaScript's Date.now() for timing and displays millisecond precision. It's accurate enough for most timing needs including workouts and productivity tracking. For scientific precision, use dedicated hardware.

### Can I save my lap times?
Yes. Click the "Copy Laps" button to copy all recorded lap times to your clipboard. You can then paste them into a spreadsheet, notepad, or any text application.

### Does the stopwatch keep running if I close the tab?
No. The stopwatch only runs while the page is open. Closing the tab or browser resets the timer. The stopwatch does not use server-side timing.

### What's the difference between split time and lap time?
Split time is the total elapsed time since the stopwatch started. Lap time is the duration since your last lap was recorded. For example, in a running workout, split time tells you your total run duration, while lap time tells you each individual mile split.

### Can I use keyboard shortcuts?
Yes. Press Space to start or stop, press L to record a lap, and press R to reset. These shortcuts make it easy to use the stopwatch without touching your mouse.
