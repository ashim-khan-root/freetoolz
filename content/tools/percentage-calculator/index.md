## FAQ

### What is the formula for "What is X% of Y?"
The formula is (X ÷ 100) × Y. For example, 15% of 200 = (15 ÷ 100) × 200 = 30. The calculator shows this formula alongside the result.

### How do I calculate percentage change?
Subtract the original value from the new value, divide by the original value, and multiply by 100. A positive result is an increase; a negative result is a decrease. The calculator handles this automatically.

### Can I use this for calculating tips?
Yes. Enter the bill amount and the tip percentage in "What is X% of Y?" mode. For an $85 bill with a 20% tip, the result is $17.00.

### Does this tool store my numbers?
No. All calculations are performed locally in your browser. Your numbers are not transmitted, stored, or logged in any way.

### Can I calculate multiple percentages at once?
This tool handles one calculation at a time. For bulk or batch percentage calculations, perform them sequentially or use a spreadsheet application.
