## FAQ

### What is PPM and why does it matter?
PPM stands for Pixels Per Meter. It measures how many pixels represent each meter of the scene. For ANPR, you need at least 125 PPM to reliably read a license plate. Higher PPM means better recognition at longer distances.

### What's the difference between detection, recognition, and identification?
- **Detection (75 ppm)** — you can see that there's a vehicle with a plate, but can't read the characters
- **Recognition (125 ppm)** — you can reliably read the plate characters (this is the ANPR standard)
- **Identification (250 ppm)** — you can read the plate with high confidence even in challenging conditions

### How does sensor size affect range?
Larger sensors (1/1.8" vs 1/3") capture more light and provide better image quality, which extends the effective ANPR range. A larger sensor with the same lens focal length will give you better low-light plate reading performance.

### What lens should I use for a 20-meter ANPR range?
For a typical 5MP camera with a 1/2.8" sensor, you'd need roughly a 25-35mm lens to achieve reliable ANPR at 20 meters. Use the calculator to dial in your exact combination.

## Tips

- Always test at the worst-case lighting condition (nighttime) when validating your ANPR setup
- Consider environmental factors — rain, fog, and dirt on the lens can reduce effective range by 20-30%
- Varifocal lenses let you adjust focal length on-site, which is helpful when the exact distance isn't known during planning
