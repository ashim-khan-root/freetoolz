## FAQ

### What is a Google Cache?
A Google Cache is a snapshot of a webpage stored on Google's servers. When Google crawls and indexes a page, it saves a copy of what it found. This cached version shows you what Google sees when it processes your page.

### How often does Google update its cache?
It varies. Popular pages with high crawl budgets may be cached every few hours. Smaller sites might see updates every few days or weeks. You can request re-crawling through Google Search Console.

### Why would I use the text-only cache?
The text-only version strips all CSS, JavaScript, and images. It lets you see exactly what textual content Google extracted from the page — useful for checking if Google is reading your content correctly.

### Is the cache different from what shows in search results?
The cache is the full page snapshot. The search result snippet is a condensed version pulled from that cached content. If your snippet looks wrong, checking the cache helps diagnose why.

### Can I view a cache from a specific date?
The standard Google Cache shows the most recent snapshot. For historical snapshots, use the Wayback Machine link provided by this tool — it maintains archives going back years.
