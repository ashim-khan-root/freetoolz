## How to Use the Rebar Calculator

1. **Enter slab dimensions** — input the length and width of your concrete slab, wall, or footing in either feet/inches or meters
2. **Set spacing** — specify the horizontal and vertical rebar grid spacing (typical spacing ranges from 12 to 24 inches)
3. **Choose rebar size** — select from #3 (10mm) through #8 (25mm) depending on your structural requirements
4. **Adjust overlap allowance** — enter the lap splice length (usually 40-60 times the bar diameter)
5. **Get results** — instantly see the number of bars needed in each direction, total linear footage, and estimated weight in pounds or kilograms

Perfect for contractors, engineers, and construction estimators. All calculations happen in your browser — nothing is uploaded.

## FAQ

### What rebar size should I use for a standard driveway?
For a 4-inch thick residential driveway, #3 (10mm) or #4 (13mm) rebar on 18-24 inch spacing is typically sufficient. Always check local building codes for specific requirements.

### How much overlap is needed for rebar splices?
Standard lap splices are typically 40 times the bar diameter for tension splices. For #4 rebar (0.5-inch diameter), that means a 20-inch overlap. Your engineer may specify different requirements.

### Does the calculator account for rebar grade?
The weight calculations use industry-standard densities that apply to Grade 40 and Grade 60 rebar. For specialty grades, the weight difference is negligible.

### Can I use this calculator for circular slabs?
This calculator is designed for rectangular slabs. For circular or irregular shapes, calculate the area manually and use the per-square-foot rebar estimate.

## Tips

- Order 5-10% extra rebar to account for cutting waste and unexpected needs
- Use epoxy-coated rebar in corrosive environments like coastal areas or parking garages
- Always verify rebar spacing requirements with a structural engineer for load-bearing applications
- Store rebar off the ground and cover it to prevent rust before pouring concrete
