## FAQ

### What does EMI stand for?
EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment — the fixed amount you pay each month to your lender until the loan is fully repaid. Each EMI covers part of the principal and part of the interest.

### How is EMI calculated?
EMI is calculated using the formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), where P is the loan principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the number of monthly installments.

### Why is the total interest so much higher than the principal for long-term loans?
On a long-term loan like a 20-year mortgage, you pay interest on the outstanding balance for the entire duration. In the early years, most of each payment goes toward interest rather than principal. This is why making extra payments early can save thousands in interest.

### Can I use this for a car loan vs home loan comparison?
Yes — the calculator works for any type of loan. Simply enter the loan amount, rate, and tenure for each scenario and compare the EMIs and total interest side by side.

### What happens if I prepay part of my loan?
Prepaying reduces the outstanding principal, which means less interest accrues going forward. This calculator shows the amortization schedule for the original terms — actual savings from prepayment depend on when and how much you prepay, plus any prepayment penalties your lender charges.
