## How to Use the Water Intake Calculator

1. **Enter your weight** — type your body weight in either pounds or kilograms. The calculator uses your weight as the primary factor in determining baseline hydration needs.
2. **Select activity level** — choose from sedentary (little to no exercise), lightly active (1-2 days/week), moderately active (3-5 days/week), very active (6-7 days/week), or extremely active (twice daily or intense training). More activity means more water needed to replace fluids lost through sweat.
3. **Choose climate** — select your typical climate: cool/moderate, warm/humid, hot/arid, or tropical. Hot and humid conditions increase water loss through perspiration, requiring additional intake.
4. **Get your target** — the calculator displays your recommended daily water intake in ounces, cups, liters, and milliliters. A suggested drinking schedule shows how to spread your intake throughout the day.
5. **Adjust as needed** — update any input to see how changes in weight, activity, or climate affect your recommended intake.

## FAQ

### How is the daily water intake calculated?
The baseline calculation uses your weight: approximately 30-40 ml per kilogram of body weight. Activity and climate adjustments add to this baseline. For example, moderate exercise adds roughly 300-500 ml per 30 minutes of activity, and hot climates add 200-500 ml depending on conditions.

### Can I drink too much water?
While rare for most people, excessive water intake can lead to hyponatremia (low blood sodium). The calculator's recommendations stay within safe ranges. If you have kidney issues, heart conditions, or take medications that affect fluid balance, consult your doctor.

### Does coffee and tea count toward my water intake?
Yes. While caffeine has a mild diuretic effect, caffeinated beverages still contribute positively to your total fluid intake. However, plain water is the most efficient hydrator and should make up the majority of your intake.

### Should I drink more when I'm sick?
Yes. Fever, vomiting, and diarrhea increase fluid loss significantly. During illness, aim for 20-30% more than your calculated target, and consider electrolyte-containing fluids like oral rehydration solutions.

### How do I know if I'm properly hydrated?
Clear or light yellow urine is a good indicator of proper hydration. Dark yellow or amber urine suggests you need more fluids. Other signs include thirst, dry mouth, fatigue, headache, and reduced urine output.

## Tips

- Keep a reusable water bottle at your desk and refill it according to your calculated schedule — seeing the bottle reminds you to drink
- Set hourly hydration goals: divide your daily target by your waking hours to create manageable hourly targets
- Add lemon, cucumber, or mint to your water if you struggle with plain water — flavor variety increases overall intake
- Drink a glass of water first thing in the morning — your body loses water overnight and starts the day dehydrated
