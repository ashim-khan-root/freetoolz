## FAQ

### What's the difference between binary (GiB) and decimal (GB)?
Decimal (base-10) uses powers of 1000: 1 GB = 1,000 MB. Binary (base-2 or GiB) uses powers of 1024: 1 GiB = 1,024 MiB. Hard drive manufacturers use decimal, while operating systems (Windows, macOS) typically report in binary. This is why a "500 GB" drive shows as ~465 GB on your computer.

### Which unit should I use for cloud storage pricing?
Cloud providers (AWS, Google Cloud, Azure) bill using decimal (base-10) units. However, they often measure network transfer in bytes and storage in GB-months. Always check the fine print to confirm which standard they use.

### How many MB are in a GB?
1 GB = 1,000 MB (decimal) or 1,024 MB (binary). Most consumer contexts (internet plans, phone storage) use decimal, while software and operating systems use binary.

### What about bits vs bytes?
Bits (b) are 1/8 the size of bytes (B). Internet speeds are typically measured in bits per second (Mbps), while file sizes are in bytes (MB, GB). To convert: 100 Mbps ÷ 8 = 12.5 MB/s actual download speed.

### What comes after terabyte?
Petabyte (PB), then exabyte (EB), zettabyte (ZB), and yottabyte (YB). This converter supports up to petabyte (PB), which covers most practical storage needs.
