Replacing a roof is one of the most expensive home improvements. Accurate material estimation saves money and prevents project delays. Here's how to calculate everything you need.

## Understanding Roof Measurements

Roofing materials are measured in "squares." One square equals 100 square feet of roof surface. A typical 2,000 square foot home needs about 20-25 squares of roofing.

## How Roof Pitch Affects Area

Roof pitch is the angle of your roof, expressed as rise over run (e.g., 6/12 means the roof rises 6 inches for every 12 inches of horizontal run).

Steeper roofs have more surface area than flat ones. Our [roofing calculator](/tools/roofing-calculator/) accounts for this automatically. The formula is:

`Roof Area = Building Area x Pitch Factor`

| Pitch | Factor | Example (2,000 sq ft) |
|-------|--------|----------------------|
| 3/12 | 1.03 | 2,060 sq ft |
| 6/12 | 1.12 | 2,240 sq ft |
| 9/12 | 1.25 | 2,500 sq ft |
| 12/12 | 1.41 | 2,820 sq ft |

## How Many Shingle Bundles?

Standard 3-tab asphalt shingles come in bundles, with 3 bundles covering 1 square (100 sq ft).

- 1 square = 3 bundles
- Each bundle covers about 33.3 sq ft
- Always add 10-15% for waste, especially on roofs with valleys, hips, and ridges

## Other Materials You'll Need

| Material | Coverage |
|----------|----------|
| Underlayment (15 lb felt) | 4 squares per roll (400 sq ft) |
| Ridge cap | 1 bundle per 20-25 linear feet of ridge |
| Drip edge | Measured by linear feet along roof edges |
| Roofing nails | About 320 nails per square |
| Flashing | Measured at valleys, chimneys, and vents |

## DIY vs Hiring a Pro

**DIY roofing saves money** on labor ($3-5 per sq ft) but requires experience, safety equipment, and physical stamina. Pros complete the job faster, include warranties, and handle disposal.

Consider DIY for small sheds or simple roof repairs. Hire a pro for steep roofs, multiple stories, or complex roof designs.

## Roofing Tips

- Order all materials before starting — running out mid-project delays everything
- Check local building codes for underlayment requirements
- Consider architectural shingles for better durability and curb appeal
- Don't roof in wet or windy conditions
- Rent a dumpster for old shingle disposal
