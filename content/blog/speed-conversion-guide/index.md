Speed limits, car dashboards, weather reports, and aviation all use different units of speed depending on where you are in the world. If you've ever rented a car abroad, tracked a hurricane, or wondered how fast mach 1 really is, you've needed a speed conversion.

This guide covers the four main speed units — KMH, MPH, knots, and mach — with formulas, mental math shortcuts, and real-world examples that make conversions second nature.

## The Four Speed Units

| Unit | Abbreviation | Used Where |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Kilometres per hour | km/h, KMH | Most countries worldwide (metric system) |
| Miles per hour | mph, MPH | US, UK, and a few Caribbean nations |
| Knots | kn, kt | Aviation, maritime, meteorology |
| Mach | M | Aerospace, supersonic aircraft |

## KMH to MPH: The Conversion You'll Use Most

### The Formula

```
MPH = KMH × 0.621371
KMH = MPH × 1.60934
```

### Simple Mental Math

For quick estimates without a calculator:

- **KMH to MPH:** Multiply by 0.6 (or divide by 5 and multiply by 3)
  - 100 km/h → 100 × 0.6 = 60 mph (actual: 62.1 mph)
  - 50 km/h → 50 × 0.6 = 30 mph (actual: 31.1 mph)

- **MPH to KMH:** Multiply by 1.6 (or double and subtract 20%)
  - 60 mph → 60 × 1.6 = 96 km/h (actual: 96.6 km/h)
  - 30 mph → 30 × 1.6 = 48 km/h (actual: 48.3 km/h)

## Knots: Speed on Water and in the Air

Knots are used in aviation, maritime, and meteorology. One knot equals one nautical mile per hour (1.852 km/h).

### Why Knots?

The nautical mile is based on the Earth's circumference — one minute of latitude equals one nautical mile. This makes navigation calculations much simpler for pilots and sailors.

### Conversion Formulas

```
MPH = Knots × 1.15078
KMH = Knots × 1.852
Knots = MPH × 0.868976
Knots = KMH × 0.539957
```

### Weather and Knots

When you see a weather report saying "winds of 30 knots," that's about 35 mph or 56 km/h. Hurricane-force winds start at 64 knots (74 mph / 119 km/h). Tropical storm force starts at 34 knots (39 mph / 63 km/h).

## Mach Speed: Beyond Sound

Mach number is the ratio of an object's speed to the speed of sound in the surrounding medium (usually air). The speed of sound varies with temperature and altitude.

### Speed of Sound at Different Altitudes

| Altitude | Temperature | Speed of Sound |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Sea level (15°C) | 15°C / 59°F | 1,225 km/h / 761 mph / 661 knots |
| 10,000 m (cruising) | -50°C / -58°F | 1,062 km/h / 660 mph / 573 knots |
| 20,000 m | -56°C / -69°F | 1,065 km/h / 662 mph / 575 knots |

### Breaking the Sound Barrier

When an aircraft exceeds Mach 1, it creates a sonic boom — a loud thunder-like sound caused by shock waves combining. The first aircraft to break the sound barrier in level flight was the Bell X-1 (Mach 1.06, 1947, piloted by Chuck Yeager). Today, supersonic flight is restricted over land in most countries due to noise concerns.

## Practical Speed Conversion Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Renting a Car in Europe

You're from the US and rent a car in Germany. The Autobahn sign says "130 km/h." How fast is that in mph?

130 × 0.621 = **80.7 mph** — about 81 mph, well within most rental car comfort zones.

### Scenario 2: Following a Hurricane Report

A hurricane has sustained winds of 130 knots. Should you evacuate?

130 knots × 1.151 = **149.6 mph** — yes, that's a Category 5 hurricane. Evacuate immediately.

## FAQ

**How do I convert KMH to MPH quickly in my head?**
Multiply by 0.6. For 100 km/h → 60 mph. It's approximate (off by about 3%), but close enough for most driving situations.

**Why do planes use knots instead of KMH or MPH?**
Knots are based on nautical miles, which correspond to one minute of latitude. This makes navigation calculations simpler for pilots — no need to convert between distance units and speed units when plotting courses.

**What's the fastest speed limit in the world?**
The highest posted speed limit is 160 km/h (99 mph) on certain sections of highway in the UAE (Abu Dhabi to Dubai). Parts of Germany's Autobahn have no posted limit, but 130 km/h is the recommended maximum.

**How fast is Mach 1 in KMH?**
Mach 1 equals approximately 1,225 km/h at sea level (15°C). The exact value varies with temperature and altitude — it's slower at higher altitudes where the air is colder.

**Do electric cars show speed in KMH or MPH?**
They show both, depending on the market. Most digital dashboards can be switched between units. In countries using KMH, the outer numbers are KMH with smaller MPH markings inside, or vice versa.

**What's the difference between statute miles and nautical miles?**
A statute mile (used in MPH) is 1,609 metres. A nautical mile (used in knots) is 1,852 metres — based on one minute of latitude. A nautical mile is about 15% longer.

**How many knots is a strong wind?**
- Fresh breeze: 17-21 knots (20-24 mph) — small trees sway
- Strong breeze: 22-27 knots (25-31 mph) — umbrellas hard to use
- Gale: 28-33 knots (32-38 mph) — walking is difficult
- Storm: 48-55 knots (55-63 mph) — minor structural damage
- Hurricane force: 64+ knots (74+ mph) — severe damage
