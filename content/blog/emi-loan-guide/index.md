EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment — the fixed amount you pay each month to repay a loan. Whether you're taking a home loan, car loan, or personal loan, understanding how EMIs work helps you make better borrowing decisions.

## What Is EMI?

An EMI is a fixed monthly payment made by a borrower to a lender until the loan is fully paid off. Each EMI payment covers both the principal amount and the interest due for that month.

In the early years of a loan, most of your EMI goes toward interest. As the loan progresses, a larger portion goes toward the principal. This process is called amortization.

## The EMI Formula

The mathematical formula used to calculate EMI is:

**EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)**

Where:
- **P** = loan principal (amount borrowed)
- **r** = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
- **n** = loan tenure in months

### Example Calculation

For a ₹5,00,000 loan at 8.5% annual interest for 5 years (60 months):

- P = 5,00,000
- r = 8.5% ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.007083
- n = 60

EMI = 5,00,000 × 0.007083 × (1.007083)^60 / ((1.007083)^60 - 1)
EMI = ₹10,261 per month

Total payment = ₹10,261 × 60 = ₹6,15,648
Total interest = ₹6,15,648 - ₹5,00,000 = ₹1,15,648

## How Loan Amortization Works

In the early years, most of your EMI pays interest. Over time, the balance shifts toward principal.

| Year | Principal Paid | Interest Paid | Remaining Balance |
|------|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 | ₹63,574 | ₹59,553 | ₹4,36,426 |
| 2 | ₹69,289 | ₹53,839 | ₹3,67,137 |
| 3 | ₹75,519 | ₹47,608 | ₹2,91,618 |
| 4 | ₹82,313 | ₹40,814 | ₹2,09,305 |
| 5 | ₹89,724 | ₹33,403 | ₹1,19,581 |

Notice how the interest portion decreases each year while the principal portion increases.

## Factors That Affect Your EMI

### 1. Loan Amount

Higher loan amount = higher EMI. Simple — you're borrowing more, so you pay more each month.

### 2. Interest Rate

Even a small rate change has a big impact. For a ₹50 lakh home loan over 20 years:

| Interest Rate | Monthly EMI | Total Interest |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| 7% | ₹38,765 | ₹43,03,632 |
| 8% | ₹41,822 | ₹50,37,240 |
| 9% | ₹44,986 | ₹57,96,724 |

A 2% difference means ₹18 lakh more in interest!

### 3. Loan Tenure

Longer tenure = lower EMI but more total interest. For a ₹10 lakh loan at 10%:

| Tenure | Monthly EMI | Total Interest |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| 3 years | ₹32,267 | ₹1,61,613 |
| 5 years | ₹21,247 | ₹2,74,818 |
| 10 years | ₹13,215 | ₹5,85,809 |

The 10-year loan has a much lower EMI but costs over ₹3 lakh more in total interest.

## Tips for Managing Loans

1. **Compare offers** — even 0.5% matters over the loan term
2. **Prepay when possible** — reducing principal early saves the most interest
3. **Choose the right tenure** — balance affordable EMI with total interest cost
4. **Check processing fees** — these add to your upfront cost
5. **Consider part-prepayment** — many loans allow partial prepayment without penalty
